Loss of Air Pressure

STEP 1: Check Tire Pressure
Use a tire pressure gauge to measure the current pressure. Compare it to the recommended pressure found in your car's manual or on the driver's door sticker.

STEP 2: Inspect for Visible Damage
Look for punctures, cuts, or bulges on the tread and sidewalls. Check the valve stem for cracks.

STEP 3: Listen for Air Leaks
Slightly inflate the tire and listen for any hissing sounds indicating escaping air.

STEP 4: Use Soapy Water
Spray a soap and water solution on the tire. Bubbles will form where there is a leak.

STEP 5: Check the Bead Area
Ensure the tire bead is properly seated against the wheel rim without any debris.

STEP 6: Inspect the Rim
Check the rim for damage or corrosion that might cause a leak.

STEP 7: Repair or Replace
Small punctures can be repaired with a plug kit. For larger damage, replace the tire.

STEP 8: Reinflate the Tire
Inflate the tire to the recommended pressure and double-check with a gauge.

STEP 9: Monitor Tire Pressure Regularly
Check your tire pressure monthly and before long trips.

Steering Issues

STEP 1: Check Tire Pressure
Ensure all tires are inflated to the recommended pressure. Uneven pressure can cause steering problems.

STEP 2: Inspect Tires for Wear
Look for uneven wear, punctures, or bulges, which can indicate alignment issues.

STEP 3: Check Power Steering Fluid
Ensure the power steering fluid is at the correct level and check for leaks.

STEP 4: Inspect the Steering Wheel
Check if the steering wheel is centered when driving straight. If not, it may indicate an alignment issue.

STEP 5: Listen for Unusual Noises
Pay attention to squealing, groaning, or clicking sounds when turning.

STEP 6: Check for Steering Wheel Vibration
Vibrations could be due to unbalanced tires or worn suspension components.

STEP 7: Inspect the Steering Linkage
Check for loose or damaged components in the steering linkage.

STEP 8: Check Wheel Alignment
Have a professional check and adjust the wheel alignment if necessary.

STEP 9: Inspect the Steering Gear and Rack
Look for any damage or wear in the steering gear and rack.

STEP 10: Consult a Professional
If the issue persists, consult a professional mechanic for a thorough inspection.

Visible Damage

This section provides guidance on how to assess visible damage to your tires.

STEP 1: Check for Cuts and Gouges
Inspect the sidewalls and tread for any deep cuts or gouges that could compromise the tire's structure.

STEP 2: Look for Bulges or Bubbles
A bulge on the sidewall indicates internal damage to the tire, which is dangerous and requires immediate replacement.

STEP 3: Inspect Tread for Embedded Objects
Carefully check the tire tread for nails, screws, glass, or other sharp objects that may have become embedded.
